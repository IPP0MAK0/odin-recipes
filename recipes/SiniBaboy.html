Sinigang na Baboy

Description

Sinigang na Baboy is a classic Filipino sour soup made with pork and tamarind as the base flavoring. It’s hearty, tangy, and comforting, perfect for rainy days.

Traditionally served with vegetables like kangkong (water spinach), radish, and eggplant, it’s a dish that highlights the bold sour notes Filipinos love.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or ribs
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2–3 long green chilies
  • Tamarind mix or fresh tamarind
  • 8 cups water
  • Fish sauce, to taste

Steps

  1. Boil pork in water until tender, skimming off scum as needed.
  2. Add tomatoes and onion, simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Add radish, eggplant, and chilies, then continue simmering.
  4. Stir in tamarind mix (or fresh tamarind extract) until soup reaches desired sourness.
  5. Season with fish sauce. Add kangkong last before serving.
